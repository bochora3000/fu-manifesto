🚨 Manifesto for F.U. Software Development 🚨

We are uncovering the most painful, dysfunctional, and soul-crushing ways to build software.

Through our suffering, we have come to value:

🤡 Bureaucracy and manipulation over individuals and interactions 🤝

📜 Eternal, mind-numbing documentation over working software 💻

⚖️ Legal battles, approval chains, and corporate politics over customer collaboration 🤷

📅 A rigid, outdated, and untouchable roadmap over responding to change 🔄

That is, while the items on the right could be useful, we actively reject them to ensure maximum inefficiency, frustration, and despair.

🔥 The F.U. Principles 🔥

We pledge to uphold these 12 commandments of dysfunction:

🛑 Cover your ass first. Our highest priority is not delivering software but making sure no one can blame us when things fail.

🐌 Delay everything indefinitely. The only good software is perfect software, which means it should never ship.

🚷 Developers and businesses must never communicate. All discussions must be funneled through a labyrinth of requirement documents written by people who don’t code.

🗿 Change is evil. Everything must be decided upfront, locked in stone, and never revisited. If the market shifts, that's their problem.

🤖 Developers are disposable. They should follow orders, not think. Creativity leads to inefficiency.

📚 If it’s documented, it’s done. Whether or not the software works is irrelevant—if the documentation is 500 pages, we call it a success.

🐢 Approval processes must be excruciating. If a request doesn’t take at least six months and involves three different committees, something went wrong.

🚀 Releases should be feared. Launches only invite criticism. The true goal is an eternal beta where nothing is ever final.

👑 Decisions must be made by those farthest from the problem. Preferably by an executive who hasn't coded since the 90s.

🙉 Users are the problem, not the software. If customers struggle, it's because they’re too stupid to use our perfect system.

📊 Architecture by committee. The best designs come from endless meetings, PowerPoints, and consultants who have never shipped a product.

⚰️ Failure is an opportunity… to add more processes. Every sprint should end with a post-mortem on why the team failed, followed by adding more bureaucracy to guarantee even worse failure next time.

🚫 Agile is for weaklings. True professionals drown in red tape, worship process over progress, and take pride in how much pain they can inflict on themselves and their teams.

💀 Welcome to the F.U. Era. 💀
